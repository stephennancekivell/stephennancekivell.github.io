--- layout: post title: Earthquake data! date: '2012-07-05T02:39:00.000-07:00' author: Stephen Nancekivell tags: modified_time: '2012-09-22T05:12:54.542-07:00' thumbnail: http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Q3vPJwCnZZA/UF2q-HZkJDI/AAAAAAAAAZA/r2FtLlJqbZE/s72-c/quake.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2519344180739427574.post-8730436643930334084 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.stephenn.com/2012/07/earthquake-data.html --- I was in my first big earthquake the other day. Being my first I didn't know if it felt like a big one or not. I jumped on geonet.org.nz looking for the answer.

GeoNet have this awesome heat map of earthquakes around NZ


I thought it'd be cool to make a video of it. To show how that changed over time. So I did.

Unfortunately I've only got the aftermath. But I'm sussed to make cool videos next time.
All I had to do was download that png a million times. Then make the video with mencoder like this. 
mencoder "mf://*.png" -mf fps=10 -o output.avi -ovc lavc -lavcopts vcodec=mpeg4
